Mashed Potatoes Johnson's Way

Description

This recipe came down from my Grandfather, Bill Jowers. In proper old school, southern cooking fashion, it is really more of a guide
than a set of exact measurements. This recipe shies away from the usual form normally associated with traditional smashed spuds and
showcases a bit of true southern spirit to create a side that is both deliciously spiced as well as soul-soothingly rich. This take on
mashed potatoes the reason that food ever came to be described with words like "soul" and "comfort".

Ingredients

  • Potatoes. Russet and Red work great with this recipe. I like to blend the two
  • Butter
  • Cavender's grill seasoning
  • Salt
  • Milk
  • Mayonnaise

Steps

  1. Rinse potatoes and rough dice into pieces about 1 inch cubed.
  2. Place potatoes in large pot and add water until level is about 2 inches higher than potatoes
  3. Boil potatoes until they can be easily pierced with a fork
  4. Drain potatoes. Put the potatoes back into the pot and place back onto burner
  5. Gently shake the pot to release excess moisture and allow it to steam off. Approx. 1 min
  6. Turn off burner and add one stick of butter per 2.5 lbs of potatoes
  7. Add 1/4 cup of milk per stick of butter
  8. Gently mash potatoes together with butter and milk until butter is melted
  9. Add mayo by the TBLS until desired consistency is reached
  10. Apply Cavender's and salt to taste
  11. Enjoy!
