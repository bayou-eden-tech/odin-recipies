Louisiana Dirty Rice

Description

You can't say "Lousiana cooking" without conjuring up images of a certain set of foods. Among them, boiled crawfish, gumbo, and of course, dirty rice.
Dirty rice may be one of the most versatille foods in the Cajun arsenal. It is sometimes refered to as "rice dressing", as it often is used to stuff turkey,
chicken, quail, and bell peppers. It can stand alone as a main dish, or blend in to a table of side dishes at your next potluck. But like all cuisine rooted in the
south, this dish is packed full of savory flavor and always a crowd pleaser.

Now, anybody can slap together rice and meat, but to really capture the essence of the dish we must include two very important features. First, the holy trinity of
southern cooking: onion, bell pepper, and celery. Second, something to give texture and cohesion to the dish. Nobody likes chasing dry, scattered rice around their plate.
For this version, we will be using bacon grease and flour to create out binding agent, and pack in a bit of extra flavor. As always, southern cooking has as many variations
as it does families who make it, so feel free to experiment with you own twist on this classic dish to make it truly your own.

Ingredients

  • white rice
  • ground beed
  • ground sausage
  • bell pepper
  • onion
  • celery
  • chicken broth
  • flour
  • cooking oil (I prefer bacon grease, but peanut oil is also a good choice)
  • spices: chili powder, oregano, parsley, thyme, paprika, bay leaves,
    garlic, salt, pepper, cayenne

Steps

Apart from boiling the rice, this entire recipe can be done in a single cast iron skillet,
stock pot, or deep skillet. Use what works best for you.

  1. Cook you rice and set aside
  2. In your main skillet, heat bacon grease or oil in your skillet over medium heat. Add ground beed and sausage
    and mix them together as they begin to brown
  3. Add the holy trinity (diced onion, celery, and bell pepper)
  4. Stir in all of the spices, except for the parsley. This is a great time to savor the aromas!
  5. Sprinkle a bit of flour over the meat. Use enough to coat the meat, but not so much that you get
    clumps of cooked flour
  6. Stir in the chicken broth and allow to simmer for 5-8 minutes
  7. Fold in the cooked rice and parsley. Simmer for another 5 minutes
  8. Serve and enojoy!
