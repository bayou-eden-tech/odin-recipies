Mama's Crawfish Etouffee

Description

We all have foods that are deeply attached to our childhood memories. For some, that may be grilled cheese or dinosaur-shaped chicken nuggets.
But if you had a southern mom, it was more likely dirty rice or etouffee. This was certainly the case for me! Though this southern classic can be made entirely
from scratch, an ingenious mother with kids to feed can make smart use of the soup aisle at the grocery store to take some of the pain out of providing her family with a quality dinner. This recipe will
be of exactly that type.

Etouffee is a savory, tomato-based sauce served over rice. It can be done with shrimp or crawfish, and is sure to be a family favorite in the southern comfort-food arsenal.

Ingredients

  • 1 stick of margerine
  • chopped bell pepper
  • chopped onion
  • 1 can cream of mushroom soup
  • 1 can celery soup
  • 1 can diced Rotel tomatoes
  • 2-4 lbs cooked crawfish or shrimp (peeled and deveined)

Steps

  1. Melt butter in large, deep-walled skillet
  2. Saute onions and bell pepper
  3. Add both cans of soup and the tomatoes (if you want to hide the veggies from picky kids or a "rice and gravy"
    kind of dad, feel free to put this mix in the blender to liquify into a rich and hearty sauce)
  4. Cook for 10 minutes
  5. Add crawfish or shrimp and simmer incovered for 15-25 minutes
  6. Serve over rice and enojy!
